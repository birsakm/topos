**Reference anchor.** The chrome-look polished aluminum inlet cowl ring at the front of a PW1100G or CFM LEAP nacelle — the smoothly rounded torus-like lip that flares outward from the inner fan-duct surface to the outer nacelle skin. On real engines this is a single forged/spun-aluminum ring that gets a polished mirror finish for anti-icing visibility.

- Geometry strategy: rotational sweep (lathe) of a rounded D-profile cross-section about the engine Y-axis. Cross-section is a quarter-ellipse ~12 cm radial × ~18 cm axial blending the inner fan-duct ID into the outer nacelle OD. Resulting ring is a smooth torus-segment, full 360°.
- Mandatory features: continuous smooth fillet (no sharp edges at the front face), inner surface flush with FanCase ID, outer surface flush with Nacelle OD, faint annular seam line where it bolts onto the nacelle (recessed groove ~3 mm).
- Color: polished chrome-look aluminum (more reflective than the Nacelle body).
- Bbox constraint: this ring sits at the most -Y end of the engine; its front face defines the engine's front-most plane.
- ANTI-pattern: do NOT model as a flat washer or a thin annulus — the lip has visible axial depth and a rounded cross-section.
