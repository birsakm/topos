**Reference anchor.** PW1000G first-stage fan rotor disk — a single forged titanium hub with 18 dovetail (or fir-tree) blade-attachment slots cut radially into the rim. The hub is thick at the center and tapers thinner toward the rim where the blade roots attach.

- Geometry strategy: rotational sweep of a thick-disk profile about the engine Y-axis to produce a forged-hub shape, then bmesh-boolean 18 evenly-spaced radial dovetail slots into the rim. OD ~55 cm at the slot bottom, rim thickness ~12 cm axially, central hub thickness ~20 cm.
- Mandatory features: 18 dovetail (trapezoidal) slot pockets cut radially into the rim at 20° spacing — these are where the FanBlade roots mate. Central bore for the LP shaft (~12 cm diameter). Two visible balance-weight pockets on the front face.
- Color: machined titanium silver with faint heat tint near the slot roots.
- ANTI-pattern: do NOT model as a smooth featureless disk — the radial dovetail slots are the defining feature that show where each fan blade attaches.
