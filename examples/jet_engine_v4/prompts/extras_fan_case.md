**Reference anchor.** Modern turbofan fan containment case (the Kevlar/composite-wrapped ring that surrounds the fan blades and contains a blade-out event). On a PW1100G this is a ~210 cm ID Kevlar-wrapped composite barrel with visible circumferential wrap layers and bolted longitudinal stator-vane attachment flanges.

- Geometry strategy: hollow thin-wall cylinder (ID ~209 cm sized to clear the FanBlade tip diameter by 3-5 mm, OD ~215 cm, length ~50 cm) sitting just behind the IntakeLip and concentric with the engine axis. Sits inside the Nacelle.
- Mandatory features: visible inner-surface abradable rub-strip (slightly different color band ~3 cm wide on the ID at the fan blade tip plane), circumferential bolt-flange ring at the aft end (~6 mm proud, 24 visible bolt heads), Kevlar-wrap texture cue (faint helical lines on the OD).
- Color: aluminum-gray OD, off-white or pale-tan abradable strip on the ID.
- ANTI-pattern: do NOT make this a featureless thin ring — the abradable strip and bolt flange are the recognizable visual cues.
