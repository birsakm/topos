**Reference anchor.** Two-stage high-pressure turbine on a PW1100G — directly downstream of the combustor and the hottest section of the engine. Two rotor disks separated by a stator ring, with single-crystal nickel-superalloy blades that are HEAVILY heat-discolored: charcoal-black with burnt-orange and straw-yellow tinting concentrated at the blade leading edges and disk rims. Air-cooling holes visible on the blades.

- Geometry strategy: two thin disks along the engine axis (each ~8 cm thick, OD ~45 cm), separated by ~6 cm with a stator vane ring between them. Each disk has a rim of ~60 small radial turbine blade stubs (extruded cambered aerofoils, span ~5 cm, chord ~3 cm, with visible cooling-hole pattern on the blade surfaces). Single stator ring between the two rotor disks with its own ~60 vanes. Outer casing cut away on the same azimuthal sector as the nacelle.
- Mandatory features: 2 rotor disks + 1 stator ring visible, heavy heat discoloration (charcoal + burnt orange + straw yellow), cooling-hole pattern hint on the blade surfaces (small punched dots or surface displacement), shroud band at the blade tips on each stage.
- Color: charcoal black base with burnt-orange and straw-yellow heat tinting concentrated at blade leading edges and disk rims. Hottest section of the engine — most discolored.
- ANTI-pattern: do NOT use the same color as the HPC. The HPT is dramatically discolored — that's its defining visual cue.
