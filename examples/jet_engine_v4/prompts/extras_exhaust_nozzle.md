**Reference anchor.** Engine exhaust nozzle ring at the aft end of a modern turbofan nacelle — the dark gunmetal ring that forms the actual exit plane of the core exhaust, slightly converging in cross-section (subsonic nozzle), with a clean machined trailing edge.

- Geometry strategy: rotational sweep about the engine Y-axis to produce a thin-walled annular nozzle ring. Inner radius ~25 cm at the upstream end converging to ~22 cm at the trailing edge; outer radius matching the inner aft-end of the Nacelle. Axial length ~25 cm.
- Mandatory features: visible slight convergence (ID shrinks aft), thin trailing-edge lip (~2 mm thick at the exit plane — the trailing edge should be sharp, not rounded), bolted flange ring at the upstream end where it mates to the Nacelle, faint heat-streaking pattern radiating from the centerline (longitudinal staining marks).
- Color: dark gunmetal / soot-stained Inconel.
- ANTI-pattern: do NOT model as a flat washer or a constant-diameter cylinder. The convergence and sharp trailing edge are the distinguishing features.
