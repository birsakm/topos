**Reference anchor.** Engine exhaust plug / tail cone on a modern turbofan — a smooth tapered nickel-alloy cone that closes out the inner annular gas path behind the LPT, sooty matte-black from exhaust deposits, with a couple of annular cooling-air slots near the upstream end.

- Geometry strategy: rotational sweep of a smooth cone-to-blunt-tip profile about the engine Y-axis. Length ~60 cm, base diameter ~50 cm at the upstream end (matching LPT last-stage ID), tapering to ~8 cm at the aft tip (slight blunt-rounding at the tip — not a sharp point). Use a gentle ogive curve rather than a straight cone.
- Mandatory features: smooth ogive taper (NOT a straight cone), 2 annular cooling-air slots ~5 mm wide near the upstream end (recessed grooves around the cone), slight blunt rounding at the aft tip, mounting flange ring at the upstream base (~6 mm proud).
- Color: matte black with heavy soot darkening, slightly lighter at the upstream end where it's recently been wiped down by hot gas flow.
- ANTI-pattern: do NOT model as a sharp straight cone. The ogive curvature, the cooling slots, and the blunt tip are all signature details.
