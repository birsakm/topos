**Reference anchor.** Annular combustor outer casing on a modern turbofan (PW1100G TALON-X combustor or CFM TAPS combustor) — a heat-resistant nickel-alloy ring chamber sitting between the HPC exit and the HPT inlet, with a ring of 16 fuel-nozzle bosses around the upstream face and rainbow-oxidation heat discoloration on the visible exterior surface.

- Geometry strategy: an annular chamber formed by two concentric cylinders (outer OD ~55 cm, inner ID ~30 cm, axial length ~25 cm) closed at front and back by annular end-walls. The chamber is hollow; on the cutaway side, remove a ~120° azimuthal wedge of the outer wall so the inside of the combustion chamber is visible.
- Mandatory features: 16 fuel-nozzle boss locations on the upstream end-wall at 22.5° spacing (these are where the FuelNozzle template instances mount — each boss is a raised cylindrical pad ~5 cm OD with a 2 cm central bore), bolted flange rings at both axial ends, rainbow heat oxidation on the visible exterior (achieved via vertex coloring or material setup), interior surface showing sooty dark coloring on the cutaway side.
- Color: nickel-alloy Inconel base color with rainbow-tint heat oxidation (blues, purples, straw-yellows) on the outside; sooty dark gray inside on the visible interior.
- ANTI-pattern: do NOT model as a closed solid drum. The cutaway and the fuel-nozzle boss ring are the defining cutaway features.
