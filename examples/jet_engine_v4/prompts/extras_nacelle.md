**Reference anchor.** Pratt & Whitney PW1100G-JM nacelle in cutaway-display configuration (think aerospace museum cutaway, e.g. the cutaway PW1100G at the Smithsonian or the manufacturer's trade-show display unit). Outer barrel is a smooth aluminum-gray composite fairing; a ~120° azimuthal sector down one side has been cut away to expose the internal core, and the cut edge has a clean machined trim line revealing the nacelle skin thickness.

- Geometry strategy: start from a hollow cylinder shell (OD ~225 cm, ID ~215 cm — wall ~5 cm to suggest skin + acoustic liner), length ~340 cm. Apply a bmesh boolean to remove a ~120° azimuthal wedge along one side from front to back, exposing the interior. Leave the front and back rings intact (full 360°) for ~10 cm at each end so the structure still reads as a barrel.
- Mandatory features: cutaway sector exposing the engine core, visible nacelle skin thickness at the cut edge (acoustic-liner honeycomb texture optional), longitudinal riveted seam line on the un-cut side, slight conical taper at the rear (~5% diameter reduction over the last 80 cm), small recessed thrust-reverser actuator boss on the upper-outer surface.
- Color: matte aluminum-gray with a faint bluish tint typical of polished aerospace aluminum.
- ANTI-pattern: do NOT model as a closed solid cylinder — the cutaway sector is the whole point. Do NOT make wall thickness zero (the cut edge must reveal a non-zero skin).
