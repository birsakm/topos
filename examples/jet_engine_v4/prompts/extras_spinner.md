**Reference anchor.** The white pointed nose-cone in front of the fan disk on a PW1100G or LEAP-1A — a smoothly tapered glossy-white cone with a single black spiral or comma marking that becomes a visible motion-blur swirl when the engine is spinning (bird/ground-crew safety marker).

- Geometry strategy: rotational sweep of a tangent-ogive profile about the engine Y-axis. Length ~40 cm, base diameter ~50 cm (matching FanDisk OD), tip at the most -Y point. The profile should curve smoothly (not a straight cone) — use a tangent-ogive or elliptical-cone equation rather than a linear taper.
- Mandatory features: smooth ogive curvature (NOT a straight-walled cone), small painted spiral marker stripe (~2 cm wide black stripe wrapping ~270° around the spinner from base to tip), flush mounting flange at the aft base (no visible bolts on the visible side — they're under the cone).
- Color: glossy white with a black spiral stripe.
- Bbox: must clear FanCase ID by at least 1 cm at the base.
- ANTI-pattern: do NOT model as a straight cone. The ogive curvature is what distinguishes a modern fan spinner from a 1960s engine.
